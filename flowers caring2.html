How to take care of flowers?

1-Watering

  1. Water the rose bushes with plenty of water instead of watering them in small amounts frequently
  2. Use the correct watering can
  3. Water the soil until the water reaches a depth of 45 centimeters
  4. Water the roses in the early morning
  5. Water the roses immediately if they begin to wilt

2-Sun Exposure

Flowers should be exposed to sunlight on a daily basis
Consider annual weather conditions. Obviously, plants need watering during hot,
dry weather, but know that wind can dry out plants a lot even in cold weather;
Newly planted roses may be at risk of drying out during autumn or the winds of winter

3-Standards of growing flowers in pots

In order to grow flowers in pots and pots, a set of criteria
must be observed for the proper growth of the plant

  1. Choosing the right utensil, preferably a pottery or glass vessel
  2. Making holes 50 cm in width and 40 cm in depth, this depth is very necessary for the growth of roots
  3. Add a little compost into the pits
  4. The addition of root stimulators, which are substances sold in nurseries, stimulate rapid root growth
  5. The sapling should be watered at the end, with kicking over the soil to set

4-Type Of Soil

Soil is an essential and important element in the success of what is being cultivated,
due to its direct impact on plant growth. It is necessary to provide good agricultural soil
rich in nutrients to ensure the growth of cultivated plants. Therefore, it is necessary
to study the properties of the soil and the surface water levels in the sites to be planted.
Good agricultural soil is required to have the following specifications: pH (about 7, EC) of dissolved salts in the soil solution is less than 2500 μmoz/cm at 25 °C,
chlorides are less than 200 ppm, calcium carbonate is less than 5% by weight of the dried soil
